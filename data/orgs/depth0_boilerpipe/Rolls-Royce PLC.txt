Rolls-Royce is the world’s preeminent manufacturer of highly-efficient integrated power and propulsion solutions. Our power systems are used in aerospace, marine, energy and off-highway applications. In the United States, Rolls-Royce employs over 6,000 people, with significant operations in 27 states. In Canada, we employ over 1,000 people across six provinces. 
Rolls-Royce in the USA
We design, develop, manufacture and test key products – from jet engines for the U.S. Department of Defense to commercial aerospace markets. Many of these engines are modified for Naval Marine Markets.
Our U.S. operations support an additional 52,000 jobs and contribute over $8.6 billion to the economy.
Rolls-Royce has invested over $1 billion in new facilities, capital or made investments to modernize its facilities in the U.S. For example, in Indianapolis, the company is in the middle of a 5-year, $600 million investment to modernize its facilities and technology upgrades.
Our reciprocating engines brand, 
MTU America
, a Rolls-Royce company, makes diesel engines at advanced manufacturing centers in Aiken, South Carolina, and Mankato, Minnesota.

 to see the contribution that Rolls-Royce makes to the U.S. economy, with a map of some of our key locations.
Rolls-Royce in Canada
In 1947, Rolls-Royce Montreal Limited was the first Rolls-Royce repair and overhaul facility built outside of the UK. Today, over 900 people provide repair, maintenance and overhaul services for our customers around the world.
In Peterborough, Ontario, Rolls-Royce operates two facilities. A marine facility provides capabilities in integrated power and propulsion systems for the naval industry. Another designs, and manufactures advanced systems for nuclear power plants.
Because of its uniquely cold winters, Rolls-Royce puts its new engine variants through a variety of cold weather testing, either on a flying test aircraft or its joint partnership of GLACIER, a static test bed located in Thompson, Manitoba.
Customers
In North America, Rolls-Royce serves many customers in a wide range of markets.
Manufacturing centers that produce engine components, whole engine assembly and test facilities from the Deep South in the U.S. to the far north in Canada.
Engineering, design and manufacturing of gas turbine and diesel powered engines.
Marine propulsion systems for commercial and naval customers.
Delivering a wide-ranging capability across the nuclear cycle, from concept design to through-life support and life-extension.
Public-Private Partnerships
Rolls-Royce believes that its relationships with customers, our communities, our academic partners and our employees are critical to our success.
America is a hotbed for innovation and the reason why we pumped over $935 million into research and development in the U.S. since 2012.
Much of this R&D activity takes place with our academic research partners – top engineering and aerospace universities – including Purdue, Virginia Tech and the University of Virginia. We also conduct research with U.S. Defense Laboratories, Air Force Research Laboratory, the Office of Naval Research as well with NASA and the Federal Aviation Administration.

DePuy Synthes Companies California Compliance
NOTICE: this information is provided pursuant to requirements of Cal. Health & Safety Code § 119402 (1765), which requires certain pharmaceutical and medical device companies doing business in California to make available their program for compliance with applicable federal and state laws and industry standards regulating marketing and promotion of their products

INTRODUCTION
Operating Companies of DePuy Synthes, under legal entity DePuy Orthopaedics, Inc., ("Company") have established a program designed to materially comply with applicable federal and state laws and industry standards relating to marketing and promotion of its products. Additionally, recognizes that adherence to these standards can be furthered through a compliance program that is informed by Compliance Program Guidance for Pharmaceutical Manufacturers, published by Office of Inspector General of Department of Health and Human Services ("Guide"). Guide advises that effective compliance programs are comprised of seven elements. As described below, these elements form basis of Company's program for compliance with standards regulating marketing and promotion of its products.
II.
 Overview of Compliance Program
1. Written Policies and Procedures
has written policies to assure substantial compliance with applicable laws and regulations and standards governing marketing and promotion of our products. Among these standards are recognized industry codes of conduct including Code of Ethics on Interactions with Health Care Professionals (AdvaMed Code), published by Advanced Medical Technology Association ("AdvaMed"). has established written policies that govern activities involving communicating with customers about appropriate use of our products including appropriate instruction, education, training, service and technical support required for safe and effective use of our products. also has policies governing activities involving advancement of scientific and educational activities supporting medical research and education. These policies include:
Policies on Educational Grants and Research Grants
may provide grants for specific educational purposes that benefit patients and customers. These may include, but are not limited to, continuing medical and paraprofessional education programs, fellowships provided to teaching institutions and similar organizations with a demonstrated commitment to scientific and technical education, and programs operated by organizations that provide high-quality, nationally recognized patient education. Funding of educational programs will generally be provided only to organizations and institutions and not to individual practitioners.
Research grants to support customer-initiated research may be provided for programs involving research in areas of legitimate interest to company. All requests are subject to scientific review prior to funding approval.
Policy on Charitable Contributions and Patient Assistance
will consider charitable contributions and requests for patient assistance in areas of children's health, health care education, access to health care and community responsibility, consistent with policies.
Policy on Travel Expense Reimbursement for CME, Promotional or Product Training Meetings
will, with very limited exceptions that are in accordance with nationally recognized standards, not reimburse for travel and lodging expenses of attendees at promotional and educational programs.
may reimburse attendees for their reasonable travel and lodging expenses associated with attendance at product training meetings. Product training meetings will be conducted in locations conducive to exchange of information such as educational or conference settings. Where face-to-face or hands-on customer training is reasonably necessary for safe and effective use of Company's medical device products is required, may provide training at facilities, at independent teaching centers such as medical institutions, or in other appropriate clinical settings.
Policy on Business Meals
may occasionally offer a modest meal, consistent with standards of AdvaMed Code as part of an educational presentation or a business discussion. Venues that feature entertainment or recreation, and attendance by spouses or guests, are not permitted.
Policy on Provision of Educational and Practice-Related Items
On occasion, representatives may provide items that benefit patients or serve a genuine educational function for Health Care Professionals, consistent with standards of AdvaMed Code.
Total Annual Dollar Limit for Meals and Educational or Practice-related Items 
Each of Companies, have established an annual limit of $1,500 for meals and educational items as aggregate value of items or activities that may be provided to California health care professionals pursuant to requirements of Cal. Health & Safety Code § 119402 (1765).
Policy Prohibiting Entertainment
It is policy of not to provide Entertainment (e.g. sporting events, golf outings, concerts, hunting, etc.) to customers.
2. Assigned Compliance Officer
has appointed Health Care Compliance Officers. Our Health Care Compliance Officers have been empowered with appropriate authority to exercise independent judgment and has free and unencumbered access to senior management.
has appointed Health Care Compliance Committees. committees are comprised of Company's Health Care Compliance Officers and members of Company's management team.
3. Training.
has an annual Health Care Compliance training process that includes testing and annual certification of appropriate employees. training covers applicable guidelines governing our compliance program. Employees are also trained on consequences of failure to comply with requirements of company's compliance program.
4. Communication.
encourages open and candid discussion between management and employees regarding any compliance concerns. employees are encouraged to report their concerns to their manager, to Human Resources Department, to Law Department or to company's Health Care Compliance Officer.
5. Auditing and Monitoring. 
self-assesses and periodically audits its compliance with its policies and procedures.
6. Enforcement and Disciplinary Guidelines.
will take disciplinary actions in response to violation of company's compliance policies or procedures. will conduct a fair and diligent investigation of matters that are brought to company's attention in order to ensure consistent application of company's standards.
7. Responses To Detected Problems and Actions To Correct Issues.
requires a prompt and diligent response to potential violations of company's compliance program, including its standards regulating marketing and promotion of our products. Actions in response to detected problems may include improving policies, procedures, training, communication and monitoring or may require disciplinary action to prevent future violations.
For a copy of this California Compliance Program, please call 1-877-889-3636. Provide your name, mailing address and request that a copy of DePuy Synthes California Compliance program be sent to you.
LAW
As part of Company's ongoing efforts in area of compliance, we have developed a Comprehensive Compliance Program that is designed to comply with applicable federal and state laws and industry standards relating to marketing and promotion of our products. To our knowledge as of date of this declaration, is in compliance with our Comprehensive Compliance Program, as described here, and with California Health & Safety Code sections 119400-119402.
Dated: May 1, 2018

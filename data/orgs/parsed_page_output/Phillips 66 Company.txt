History
Our company’s name reflects our rich history, strong brand recognition and value from being one of the leading fuel brand names in the United States for almost 130 years. has had great moments that demonstrate its vision and values, and we’ve never been more excited about the future.
Origins
Conoco began as the Continental Oil and Transportation Co., one of the first petroleum marketers in the West, in 1875.
The Big Orange Ball is born
The 76 brand, long familiar in the western U.S., was created by Union Oil of California (later Unocal) in 1932.
Helping win the war
During World War II, Petroleum and Conoco made high-octane aviation fuel possible, boosting the power of Allied airplanes.
During the 50s, 60s and 70s, Conoco and Petroleum expanded operations and entered new markets. Conoco built a new refinery in Billings, Mont. and expanded overseas. invented polyethylene plastics and entered the plastics business. It also began producing a polyolefin plastic trademarked as Marlex. Wham-Manufacturing used Marlex in the production of the hula hoop.
Takeover targets
In 1981, DuPont acquired Conoco after a heated takeover battle, and Conoco became a wholly owned DuPont subsidiary. It was the largest merger in history at that time. Petroleum fended off multiple takeover attempts during the 80s as well.
Conoco and Petroleum officially merged in 2002 to become ConocoPhillips, creating the sixth-largest publicly traded oil in the world and the third-largest in the U.S.
Conocospun off its midstream and downstream businesses to create a publicly traded called that began trading under the symbol on the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) on May 1, 2012.
